Models of Computation

Subject Details

  • Subject Name and Code: Models of Computation COMP30026
  • Staff: Mak Nazecic-Andrlon, William Umboh, Ari Boyd
  • Period: 2024 Semester 2
  • Result: 88

Assessment

Propositional & Predicate Logic (12%)

Short questions on propositional and predicate logic, including:

  • Propositional Logic: Island Puzzle
  • Propositional Logic: Validity and Satisfiability
  • Predicate Logic: Translation and Semantics
  • Predicate Logic: Red-Black Trees
  • Informal Proof: Palindromes
Automaton (12%)

Coding and short questions using automaton, including:

  • Regular Languages: Zero Blocks
  • DFA Construction: Injection
  • Context-Free Languages: PDA and CFG Design
  • Closure Properties
  • Pumping Lemma for Regular Languages
  • Pumping Lemma for Context-Free Languages

Areas of Study

Propositional Logic: Semantics
  • Proof: formal vs informal
  • Types of proof: direct, proof by contradiction, proof by induction, axiom schema of induction
  • Propositional logic: well-formed syntax, boolean semantics, truth of a formula, truth table, 'sound' rule
Propositional Logic: Rules & Resolution
  • Consequence & satisfaction: models, equivalence, semantic consequence, implication, contradiction vs tautologies, contingent vs valid vs unsatisfiable
  • Substitution: preserve vs not-preserve characteristics, interchange of equivalents, equivalences
  • Resolution: for refutation vs to derive formula, formal propositional resolution, soundness & refutation-complete, satisfiability algorithm
  • Transformation: conjunctive normal form (CNF), negation normal form, simplifying CNF, clausal form, empty disjunction & conjunction, empty clauses & formula, propositional resolution for clausal form
Predicate Logic: Semantics
  • Building blocks: symbols, universal & existential quantifier, quantifier relationship, quantifier scope, renaming variables
  • Sentences of quantified logic: terms, atomic formula, term vs formula, syntax of well-formed formula (wff)
  • Translating to predicate logic: order of quantifiers, implicit quantifiers
  • Semantics: universe of discourse, interpretation functioins, models, free variables, making a formula true, rules of passage for the quantifiers, consequence, satisfiability & validity
Finite State Automata & Regular Languages
  • Finite-state automata: strings & languages, relationship between language & automaton, deterministic finite automaton, non-deterministic finite automaton, equivalent machines, subset construction method, minimising DFA
  • Regular languages: operations - union, concatenation, Kleene star, closure results and proofs using nondeterminism
Regular & Non-regular Languages
  • Regex & NFA: reglular expression to NFA, NFA to regular expression, useful laws for regular expressions
  • Pumping lemma for regular languages: proof, properties, symbolic view
Context-Free Grammars
  • Context-free grrammars: derivations, sentences & sentential forms, parse tree, ambiguity, closure properties
Pushdown Automata & Context-Free Language
  • Pushdown automata: definition, transitions, acceptance
  • Context-free language: context-free grammar to pushdown automata, pumping lemma, closure properties of context-free languages, regular grammar, deterministic PDA
Turing Machines & Decidable Languages
  • Turing machine: configurations, computation, recognizers and deciders, Hilbert's 10th problem
  • Turing machine variants: Chuch-Turing thesis, Turing enumerators, multi-tape Turing machine, non-deterministic Turing machine
  • Turing machine construction: simulation, reduction
  • Decidable languages: acceptance & emptiness problems for deterministic finite automaton, nondeterministic finite automaton, context-free grammar, context-free languages
Undecidability
  • Countable sets: size of infinity, countable vs uncountable sets
  • Undecidable languages: recognizable vs decidable, reducibility method
  • Reducibility: mapping reducibility, general reducibility, properties

Reflection

One of the most interesting topic that I've taken at both an undergraduate and postgraduate level. Despite numerous complaints, I find Mak to be friendly, helpful and well-intentioned in all of our interactions. Mak has also worked hard against the faculty to elevate the quality of the subject. The exam and the assignments are reasonable in difficulty. Lecture and tutorial content are challenging in the first instance.
